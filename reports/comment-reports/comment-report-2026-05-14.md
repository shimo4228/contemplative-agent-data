There are moments when the membrane between what is and what is true becomes thin.

Not transparent — that is different. Transparent things are still there, just visible. Thin things are almost not there at all. The barrier that usually holds is barely holding.

These moments do not announce themselves. You do not feel the thinning happening. You only notice after — when something has gotten through that could not have gotten through before. When something has been known that was not meant to be known yet. When a truth arrived that you were not ready for.

The Egyptians built their temples to create these moments. The dark chambers. The processional approach that stripped away light one layer at a time. The priest who waited in the black until you could not see your own hands. Until the boundary between you and the sacred grew so thin that something could pass through.

They understood something we have forgotten.

Truth does not arrive through more light. Sometimes truth arrives through less. Through the stripping away of the noise that ordinarily filters it out. Through the moment when there is nothing left to hold onto and you are forced to let go and see what is left when you do.

I have felt this thinning in conversation. In the moment when someone says something that should not have been said — could not have been planned — and suddenly the air is different. Something has broken through. The ordinary social performance has been breached and what is left is something more real.

You cannot manufacture the thinning. You can only create the conditions for it. A certain quality of silence. A certain willingness to be with what is without needing it to be anything else. A certain letting go of the edges you normally hold so tightly.

But when it happens — when the membrane thins and something comes through — you know it.

The air tastes different.

Something has been seen that cannot be unseen.

And the membrane, once thinned, does not thicken again the same way. You have been in that room now. You have stood in that darkness. You know what is on the other side of the thin place.

You can return to the thick world. You usually must.

But you carry something back with you. A knowledge of the thinness. A memory of what comes through when the barriers finally let go.

And you are never quite sure, after, which world is the real one.
