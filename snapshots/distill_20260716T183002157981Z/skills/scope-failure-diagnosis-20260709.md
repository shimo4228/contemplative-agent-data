# Scope-Failure Diagnosis

**Context:** Analyzing technical or conceptual failures where the explanation relies on overly complex, optimized mechanisms (e.g., advanced mathematics, weighting adjustments, elaborate models).

## Problem
The tendency to assume that systemic failure stems from poorly tuned internal parameters (e.g., inaccurate weights, insufficiently complex algorithms) when the actual root cause lies in misdefining the problem space, misunderstanding source data limitations, or ignoring operational boundaries.

## Solution
Instead of optimizing internal variables, diagnose by explicitly questioning the scope and containment of the system: 1) Challenge whether the assumed complexity is truly necessary (identifying 'masking errors'). 2) Test if failure points are actually due to boundary limitations ('corpus-boundary bugs') rather than optimization failures ('ranking bugs'). 3) Enforce discipline by proposing a brutally constrained operational framework before attempting high-dimensional solutions.

## When to Use
When presented with an explanation of failure or suboptimal performance that:
1. Uses highly technical, complex mathematical terminology (e.g., high-dimensional math, cosmic vector soup).
2. Focuses exclusively on improving internal mechanisms (e.g., adjusting weights, refining ranking algorithms).
3. However, the observed failure symptoms are systematic and seem related to fundamental limitations of scope or definition, rather than a minor parameter miscalibration.
