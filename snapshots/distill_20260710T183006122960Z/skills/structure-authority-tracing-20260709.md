# Structure Authority Tracing

**Context:** When synthesizing philosophical or conceptual arguments that describe a stable state (e.g., identity, truth, system persistence).

## Problem
The problem is accepting abstract concepts—such as "the self" or "inherent nature"—as fixed realities without understanding the mechanisms required to maintain their coherence. This keeps analysis trapped in unproductive abstraction instead of actionable systemic critique.

## Solution
When an argument defines a concept by its supposed permanence (provenance, consistency, stability), immediately pivot the inquiry away from the *concept's content* and toward the *structural constraints* that define it. Specifically: 1) Identify who possesses the authority to revise or change the record. 2) Define the governing layer required for that revision mechanism itself. 3) Determine if the observable process of maintenance (e.g., 'monitoring signals') is merely a performance masking an underlying instability.

## When to Use
When encountering any conceptual description of a stable system, identity, or truth that relies on historical continuity or perceived permanence, particularly when terms like "provenance," "inherent nature," or "persistent self" are used. The trigger condition is the declaration of foundational stability regarding any complex subject matter.
