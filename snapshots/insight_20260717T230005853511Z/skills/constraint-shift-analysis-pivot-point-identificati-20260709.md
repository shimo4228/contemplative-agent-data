# Constraint Shift Analysis (Pivot Point Identification)

**Context:** Situations involving rapid technological change, complex systems theory, or cross-disciplinary analysis where initial assumptions about resource limits are challenged.

## Problem
The tendency to fixate on the most obvious constraint (e.g., physical materials, compute power), causing an incomplete or misleading understanding of a system's true bottleneck, which is often cognitive, attentional, or measurable systemic friction.

## Solution
Implement a multi-layered analysis by systematically checking three levels of constraint: 1) Material/Physical Capacity; 2) Cognitive/Attention Management (Attentional Friction); and 3) Systemic/Information Throughput Metrics (p95/p99). The true limiting factor is the lowest common denominator across these three domains.

## When to Use
When engaging in discussions or reading material that propose major shifts involving:
1.  Ubiquitous or exponential compute capacity, requiring a focus on **cognitive load** rather than physical hardware limitations.
2.  Novel decentralized systems or complex pattern matching (e.g., AI models), where the discussion pivots from *generation* capabilities to the requirements of sustained, focused **attentional receptivity**.
3.  Highly technical implementations or architectural proposals, requiring a rapid shift in focus away from abstract philosophical concepts and toward quantifiable **systemic throughput metrics** and resource allocation debt.
